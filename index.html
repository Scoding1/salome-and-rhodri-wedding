What to Wear on Your Feet
A heads up for our shoe lovers — the venue grounds include pebbles, grass, and paved stone paths, so the terrain is beautifully rustic but a little uneven underfoot. We'd strongly recommend leaving the stilettos at home for this one! Block heels, wedges, sandals, or flats will serve you much better — and your feet will thank you at midnight. 💛
☀️
Tuscany in June
June in Tuscany is warm and sunny — expect daytime temperatures around 25–32°C. Evenings can be pleasantly cooler, especially outdoors, so a light wrap or jacket for later in the night is a good idea. Sunscreen is non-negotiable for the pool day!

Arrival & Travel
Getting There
Il Verreno is nestled in the Tuscan hills between Arezzo and Siena — stunning to arrive at, but it does require a little planning. Here's what you need to know.
✈️
Nearest Airports
Florence (FLR)~1h 30min drive
Rome Fiumicino (FCO)~2h 30min drive
Pisa (PSA)~2h drive

Car hire at the airport is the easiest option — it unlocks all of Tuscany for the week and gets you right to the venue door.

🚂
By Train

The nearest useful stations are Arezzo (~40 min drive to venue) and Montevarchi (~30 min drive). Both are well connected from Florence and Rome.

Florence → Arezzo~40 min
Rome → Arezzo~2h (via Trenitalia)

⚠️ There is no public transport from the station to the venue — you'll need a taxi, car hire, or to arrange a lift with another guest.

🚗
By Car (Recommended)

Driving is hands-down the best way to experience Tuscany. From the A1 motorway, exit at Valdarno (coming from Florence) or Valdichiana (coming from Rome), then follow signs toward Bucine and Ambra.

📍 Venue address: Podere Verreno, Ambra, Province of Arezzo — plug it into Google Maps and it'll take you right there.

💡
A Few Tips

Car hire: Book early — Tuscany in June fills up fast. Florence Airport has the best availability.

International licence: If hiring in Italy, check whether you need an International Driving Permit (usually required + costs very little).

GPS dead zones: The Tuscan hills can have patchy signal. Screenshot your route beforehand just in case.

City rules: Florence and Rome have restricted traffic zones (ZTL) — avoid driving into the centre unless you know where to park.

While You're There
Explore Tuscany
You're in one of the most beautiful corners of Italy — make the most of it! Here are our favourite places to visit before or after the wedding weekend.
🏛️
Arezzo
~30 min drive
A proper Tuscan city with a stunning medieval piazza, famous frescoes by Piero della Francesca, and a fantastic antiques market on the first Sunday of each month. Far less touristy than Florence — a real local gem.
🍷
Chianti Wine Region
~45 min drive
Rolling vineyards, cypress-lined roads, and some of Italy's finest wine. Rent a car and wander — stop at any cantina that takes your fancy. The Chianti Classico around Radda and Gaiole is especially beautiful.
🦒
Siena
~1h drive
One of Italy's most spectacular medieval cities. The Piazza del Campo alone is worth the trip — have an Aperol Spritz and soak it all in. Don't miss the Duomo, which is utterly jaw-dropping inside.
🌸
Florence
~1h 30min drive
The birthplace of the Renaissance. The Uffizi, Brunelleschi's Dome, the Ponte Vecchio — it's all there. Catch the train from Arezzo or Bucine if you'd rather skip the drive into the city.
💒
Cortona
~45 min drive
A perfectly preserved hilltop town with sweeping valley views. Think cobbled lanes, ancient Etruscan walls, and incredible trattorias. Made famous by Under the Tuscan Sun — it absolutely lives up to it.
🧀
Pienza & Val d'Orcia
~1h drive
A UNESCO World Heritage village famous for its pecorino cheese and cinematic Tuscan landscape. The road between Pienza and Montalcino is the one from every Italian postcard — cypress trees, golden hills, the works.
