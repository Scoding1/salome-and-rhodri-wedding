Region
Toscana, Italy
Il Verreno sits in the Valdambra, a quiet valley named after the Ambra stream, tucked between the hills of Chianti and the upper Arno. The area around Ambra has been inhabited since Etruscan times, and was a medieval crossroads contested between Florence, Siena and Arezzo for centuries. Today it's all rolling vineyards, cypress-lined roads and olive groves. Classic Tuscany, almost untouched. We couldn't think of a more beautiful corner of the world to get married in. 🌿

Tuscany in June
June in Tuscany is warm and sunny. Expect daytime temperatures around 25–32°C. Evenings can be pleasantly cooler, especially outdoors, so a light wrap or jacket for later in the night is a good idea.
👠
What to Wear on Your Feet
A heads up for our shoe lovers: the venue grounds include pebbles, grass, and paved stone paths, so the terrain is beautifully rustic but a little uneven underfoot, and we'd strongly recommend leaving the stilettos at home for this one! Block heels, wedges, sandals, or flats will serve you much better — and your feet will thank you at midnight. 💛

Visas & Entry Requirements
Good news — Australian and UK passport holders don't need a visa for short trips to Italy. You can stay for up to 90 days within any 180-day period across the Schengen Area (which includes most of Europe). If you're coming just for the wedding, you're all set!
📘
Passport Validity
Make sure your passport is valid for at least 3 months after your return date — that's the official rule for entering Italy and the Schengen Area. To be safe, we'd recommend having at least 6 months validity, as some airlines enforce stricter requirements. If in doubt, renew it now to avoid any last-minute stress!
Arrival & Travel
Getting There
Il Verreno is tucked in the Tuscan hills between Arezzo and Siena, stunning to arrive at, but it does need a little planning. Here's everything you need to know.
✈️
Nearest Airports
Florence (FLR)~1hr drive
Rome Fiumicino (FCO)~2h 30min drive
Pisa (PSA)~2h drive

Car hire at the airport is the easiest option, unlocking all of Tuscany for the week and gets you right to the venue door.

🚗
By Car (Recommended)

Driving is hands-down the best way to experience Tuscany. From the A1 motorway, exit at Valdarno (coming from Florence) or Valdichiana (coming from Rome), then follow signs toward Bucine and Ambra.

📍 Venue address: Podere Verreno, Ambra, Province of Arezzo. Plug it into Google Maps and it'll take you right there.

🚂
By Train

The nearest useful stations are Arezzo (~40 min drive to venue) and Montevarchi (~30 min drive). Both are well connected from Florence and Rome.

Florence → Arezzo~40 min
Rome → Arezzo~2h (via Trenitalia)

🚌 There is a local bus from Bucine station → Ambra village (Autolinee Toscane, ~16 min, every 4 hrs, Mon–Sat) but it doesn't reach the venue itself, so you'd still need a short taxi or lift from Ambra. We wouldn't rely on this as your main plan, but it's there if needed!

💡
A Few Tips

Car hire: Book early. Tuscany in June fills up fast. We'd recommend sorting this well in advance.

International licence: If hiring in Italy, check whether you need an International Driving Permit (usually required + costs very little).

GPS dead zones: The Tuscan hills can have patchy signal. Screenshot your route beforehand just in case.

City rules: Florence and Rome have restricted traffic zones (ZTL), so avoid driving into the centre unless you know where to park.

While You're There
Explore Tuscany
You're in one of the most beautiful corners of Italy, please make the most of it! Here are our favourite places to explore while you're in the area.
Arezzo
~40 min drive
Being the closest big town to the venue, Arezzo is a great option for anyone who doesn't want to travel too far. The Piazza Grande is beautiful, the Basilica di San Francesco has some genuinely famous frescoes worth seeing, and the Medici Fortress up on the hill is free to wander with great views over the valley. A really easy and rewarding half-day trip.
Chianti
~55 min drive
This region means a lot to us. Radda in Chianti was where we went on our first big trip together and it never gets old. Do a wine tour if you can, sitting on a terrace with a glass of Chianti Classico looking out over the valley is hard to beat. There are also some brilliant Tuscan cooking classes in the area if you fancy learning a thing or two. The drive through the hills alone is worth it.
Siena
~40 min drive
Sit in the Piazza del Campo with an Aperol and take it all in, it's one of those places that's even better in real life than in photos. The Cathedral is a site worth seeing. The streets around it are great for wandering and getting a bit lost.
Florence
~1hr drive
Worth at least a full day, ideally two. The Duomo, the Uffizi, Ponte Vecchio, Piazzale Michelangelo at sunset, all brilliant. A Vespa or bike wine tour is a great way to explore the nearby countryside and easily missed hidden gems. We'd also really recommend looking up a free walking tour, a great way to pick up some local knowledge and make sure you don't miss the must-sees.
